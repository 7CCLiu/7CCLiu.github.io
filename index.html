About me

I am a first year Ph.D student at the Chinese Unversity of Hong Kong (CUHK), under supervision of Prof. John C.S. Lui. I received a B.S in statistics from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2022. I am interesed in optimization and machine learning. Besides, I work closely with Prof. Luo Luo.

Research

I am learning bandits, online learning theory and Federate Optimization now. My current research is about second-order methods and I think the following topics are interesting. If you have the same interest on them, feel free to drop me an email to have a further discussion.

Topics

  • Accelerated quasi-Newton: Can we use Nesterov acceleration on the greedy/random quasi-Newton methods to accelerate the first period of the local convergence?

  • Global behavior of regularized Newton methods.

  • L-BFGS: quasi-Newton methods with limited memory are popular, can we give sharper convergence bound of them?

I was a summer intern in Prof. Tong Zhang's research group where I met prof. Luo Luo and spent my last year in USTC to do some research about quasi-Newton methods with him. We've done some interesting work about quasi-Newton methods for minimax optimzation [1, 2].
