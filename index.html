About me

I am a final-year undergraduate student at University of Science and Technology of China, majoring in Statistics. Prior to that, I got most of the credits (120/160) required for a bachelor’s degree in CS. I am interested in Optimization and Machine Learning.
I am the coming Ph.D student in 2022 fall at the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) under supervision of Prof. John C.S. Lui. Besides, I work closely with Prof. Luo Luo.

Research

I am learning bandits and online learning theory now. And I am looking for visit or research intern opportunities to do something related to optimization, online learning, multi-arm-bandits or federate learning.

I was a summer intern in Prof. Tong Zhang's research group where I met prof. Luo Luo and spent my last year in USTC to do some research about quasi-Newton methods with him. We've done some interesting work about quasi-Newton methods for minimax optimzation [1,2].

I think the following topics about the second-order methods are interesting, if you have the same interest on them, feel free to drop me an email to have a further discussion.

Topics

  • Accelerated quasi-Newton: Can we use Nesterov acceleration on the greedy/random quasi-Newton methods to accelerate the first period of the local convergence?

  • Global behavior of regularized Newton methods: Can we design efficient second-order methods which are globally convergent and faster than existing first-order methods under strongly convex and gradient & Hessian Lipschitz-continuous assumptions?

  • LBFGS: quasi-Newton methods with limited memory are popular, can we give sharper convergence bound of them?
